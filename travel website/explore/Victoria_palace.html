Overview

A sprawling marble palatial monument dedicated to the memory of Britan’s Queen Victoria, this is one of Kolkata’s most famous landmarks and was built using the same Makrana Marble as Taj Mahal.

What To Expect?

Walk around the monument and click pictures of its symmetric reflection in the nearby pond. Inside, you will find well-curated galleries with memorabilia from the British Raj era and photographs showing Kolkata’s visual history.

Tips

  • In late evenings, the multi-coloured musical fountains in the garden make for a pretty sight.
  • The well-manicured gardens are open on all days from 5:30 a.m. to 6:15 p.m.
  • Art lovers should check out the temporary exhibition section for interesting international exhibits.
  • There is an entry fee of INR 20 for Indians and INR 200 for foreigners.
  • Students with ID/uniform can tour for free.
